About the Book

Where the sea meets the storm, one woman charts her own course.

Set in 1892, Purple Immobile follows Holbird, a midshipwoman aboard the HMS Welfare, as she leaves behind a life of discrimination in England for the uncertain promise of America. But the Atlantic has other plans.

As a catastrophic hurricane barrels toward the Gulf Coast, Holbird's journey collides with the fate of Guy D. Stop, a weatherman in New Orleans watching his city crumble under nature's fury. Their stories intertwine across chess games, raging seas, and the quiet desperation of people seeking purpose in a world that offers none.

This is a story about resilience, identity, and the storms we carry within us.

Inside the Novel

Three perspectives. One hurricane. No turning back.

I

The Last Crew Member

Holbird boards the Welfare at a bustling English port, the last to step aboard. She meets Nelson the boatswain, the insufferable cook's assistant Tivda, and begins to find her footing through an unexpected game of chess.

II

New Orleans

Across the Atlantic, meteorologist Guy D. Stop tracks a monster hurricane toward an already devastated city. As the storm bears down, he confronts the futility of rebuilding and the weight of a life spent on the front lines of disaster.

III

The Calm Before the Storm

The Welfare sails into the eye of the hurricane. Holbird and Nelson share a second chess match, deeper conversations about their pasts, and a growing unease as the storm closes in around them.

The Cast

The people aboard the Welfare and the souls ashore.

Protagonist

Holbird

A midshipwoman fleeing England for a fresh start in America. Determined, guarded, and quietly fierce over a chessboard.

Boatswain

Nelson

A warm-hearted sailor with sapphire eyes and a chess set inherited from his late father. Holbird's first real friend aboard the Welfare.

Captain

Captain Key

Commanding and composed, he steers the crew through impossible weather with a quiet authority that earns universal respect.

Meteorologist

Guy D. Stop

A weatherman in New Orleans haunted by a hurricane-ravaged city. His journal entries reveal a man losing his grip on hope.

Cook's Assistant

Tivda

Cocky, insufferable, and always lurking. Nelson warned Holbird about him for good reason.

Midshipman

Robert

A disheveled fellow trainee with a cunning smirk. Independent to a fault and reluctant to collaborate.
